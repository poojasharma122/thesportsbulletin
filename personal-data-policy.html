Personal Data Policy

At The Sports Bulletin (“we,” “our,” or “us”), we take the protection of your personal data seriously. This Personal Data Policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, how we process it, and the rights you have regarding your personal information.

What Personal Data We Collect

We collect only the data necessary to operate and improve thesportsbulletin.net (“the Site”) and to provide you with the services you request. This may include:

  • Contact Information: Name and email address when you subscribe to our newsletter or contact us directly.
  • Usage Data: Information about how you interact with the Site, including IP address, device type, browser type, pages visited, and referral source.
  • Preferences: Newsletter subscription status, cookie preferences, and user settings where applicable.

We do not knowingly collect sensitive personal data (e.g., government IDs, financial data, or health information).

Why We Collect Personal Data

We collect personal data for the following purposes:

  • Content Delivery: To provide news, analysis, and fantasy football content relevant to your interests.
  • Communication: To send newsletters and respond to inquiries or feedback.
  • Analytics & Performance: To understand site traffic, improve user experience, and optimize site performance.
  • Legal Obligations: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, or law enforcement requests.

How We Process and Store Personal Data

  • Processing Basis: We process personal data under legitimate interest (site operation, analytics) and user consent (newsletter subscriptions, cookies).
  • Storage: Data is securely stored using industry-standard encryption and access controls.
  • Retention: We retain data only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined above or comply with legal obligations.

Sharing of Personal Data

We do not sell or rent personal data. We may share data with:

  • Service Providers: Trusted partners who help with email delivery, analytics, and website hosting.
  • Legal Authorities: When required by law or to protect our rights, safety, or users.

All third-party providers are required to comply with strict data protection standards.

Legal Compliance

This policy is designed to meet the requirements of GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable privacy laws. Visitors from regions requiring consent will see a cookie banner upon first visit to accept or customize preferences.

Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to:

  • Access a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Request deletion (“right to be forgotten”).
  • Restrict or object to certain types of processing.
  • Withdraw consent for email communications or cookies.

To exercise these rights, contact us at info@thesportsbulletin.net.

International Data Transfers

If you are accessing the Site from outside the country where our servers are located, your data may be transferred across borders. By using the Site, you consent to this transfer in accordance with this policy.

Data Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal data from unauthorized access, alteration, or destruction. However, no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Updates to This Policy

We may revise this Personal Data Policy from time to time to reflect changes in regulations or business practices. The latest version will always be posted on this page with the effective date clearly indicated.
